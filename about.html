About me

Hello there! Thanks for visiting my site.

I am a visual communicator that uses design and analyses to translate nuanced subjects into something that is more understandable.

In college, knowing absolutely nothing out of what I wanted in life, I landed in business school. In one of my general education classes, our professor showed us the water bottle video - you know, the one where they deconstruct the entire supply chain of where the raw materials of a plastic water bottle were collected, how they were transported, processed, all the way to being put on a shelf for a human to buy. I was absolutely mesmerized. I received a BS in Supply Chain Management from the University of Oklahoma with a minor in Spanish (one thing my upbringing in Houston left me with besides total discontent with any salsa that doesn’t come from Texas, is my interest in Spanish language and music). Then my own personal "water bottle video" life step came true and I moved to Kansas to manage a complex industrial global supply chain for a chemical company. It was invigorating and they trusted me with more responsibility than I expected. This experience helped me build a robust set of skills around decomposing complex systems, extracting the information that is useful in those systems, and using this knowledge to achieve several corporate goals seamlessly. Something was missing though, I knew as I read Orbiting the Giant Hairball. I refocused my goals, moved to the east coast, and took on a role at a communications and marketing agency for a think tank. I built comprehensive operations and financial data visualization dashboards, advised on business operations strategy, and project managed the launch of the company onto an enterprise project management software. It wasn't as challenging as my first role, but the best part was being inspired by the buzz of the crazy driven people in DC. I found out about a Data Analytics bootcamp at GW University that I took night classes for while working my day job, and 6 months later I had a certificate and a semblance of how to code in Python and build websites. Eager to use these skills, I got a job as a data visualization and front-end web developer at Booz Allen Hamilton, where I now work within Vue.js (HTML/CSS/JS), Python, Figma, Tableau, R, Adobe Illustrator, and of course the tool no consultant can go without: PowerPoint. Soon after starting this job, I started pursuing my Master's in Information Visualization at MICA in the evenings, because I wasn’t crazy enough for taking a 6 month bootcamp in my free time. I’m on a mission, and I won’t stop until I get there, and then there will still be more to learn! I'm SO excited to dig deeper into data visualization design with the tools, design knowledge and languages I've been learning so I can become a more effective visual communicator.

Outside of work and class, some of my hobbies include illustrating, rock climbing with my husband, cooking, playing with my adorable dog Pablo. I'd like to live in the mountains reeeal soon.
