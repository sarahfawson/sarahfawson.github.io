About me

Hello there! Thanks for stopping by.

I am a designer and illustrator that uses data to communicate and to delight.

In college, knowing absolutely nothing out of what I wanted in life, I landed in business school. In one of my classes, our professor showed us the water bottle video, where they deconstruct the entire supply chain of a plastic water bottle like where the raw materials were collected, how they were transported, processed, all the way to the product being put on a shelf for a human to buy. I was absolutely mesmerized. I switched my major to Supply Chain Management and got my Bachelors degree from the University of Oklahoma with a minor in Spanish. I then moved to Kansas to start my first job, managing the supply chain for a product line at a chemicals company, whose materials make things like airbags, carpet, and lululemon fabric. This experience helped me build skills around decomposing complex systems, extracting the information that is useful in those systems, and using this knowledge to achieve several goals seamlessly.

It was invigorating, but something was missing in my career, I knew as I read Orbiting the Giant Hairball. I needed more creativity in my life. I refocused my goals, moved to the east coast, and started working on the steps to live a more creative life. This came through different corporate jobs, more school, and lots of side hustling. All I can say now is that the journey continues.

Outside of work, some of my hobbies include illustrating, hiking, rock climbing with my husband, cooking, and playing with my dog Pablo.
